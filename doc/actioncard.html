The ActionCard

Click on a section of the ActionCard below to see its description.


Wrestler Name

The name of the wrestler

General Card

The General Card varies with respect to the wrestler's ability. The General Card is used to determine if a wrestler is on offense or defense. On the General Card, dice totals ranging from 2-12 are shown with two potential results:
  1. OC = Wrestler needs to roll on Offensive Card
  2. DC = Wrestler needs to roll on Defensive Card

Defensive Card

The Defensive Card lists the possible defensive actions a wrestler can take. Again dice totals ranging from 2-12 are represented with the following possibilities:

  • A = Wrestler attempts to break the opponent's offensive move but are unsuccessful

  • B = Wrestler simply absorbs the damage of the opponent's offensive move

  • C = Wrestler effectively neutralizes the opponent's offensive move

  • REVERSE = Wrestler counters the opponent's offensive move and immediately becomes the offensive wrestler


Specialty

The Specialty Card lists the wrestler's most effective move and the possible point totals when the specialty move is executed. In addition to the point totals in the list, it's possible that the move could result in a submission opportunity (denoted by a "*") or a pin attempt (denoted by P/A).

SUB

If the wrestler is the victim of a submission hold, the range of numbers listed here comes into play. When a submission roll is made, if the result falls into the range of numbers listed, the wrestler submits.

TAG-TEAM

The TAG-TEAM factor is used when, during a tag team match, the wrestler's partner is in a pinning predicament or a submission hold. If the situation is dire the wrestler will attempt to make a save. If the saving roll falls within the range of numbers listed, the wrestler will make the save.

PRIORITY

These are the ratings of the wrestler in singles and tag team matches. The first number is the rating for singles matches and the second number is the rating for tag matches. The ratings are broken down as follows:

5+ Championship Caliber

Offensive Card

The Offensive Card lists the moves employed by the wrestler. For each offensive move there is a point total which indicates the effectiveness of the move. There are also moves without point totals such as ROPES, Specialty moves (denoted by a "(S)"), and Disqualification moves (denoted by a "(DQ"). ROPES means that the player will need to roll on the ROPES card. A specialty move means that the player will need to roll on the Specialty card. A disqualification move, means that the wrestler has thrown the opponent out of the ring and could potentially win by countout.

An offensive move can also be a submission hold (denoted by a "*") or a move that leads to a pin attempt (denoted by a "P/A").

The (XX) is used for Grudge Matches which are currently not implemented.


ROPES

The ROPES section lists various rope manuevers and their corresponding point totals. An "NA" means there was No Action at the ropes, which results in 0 points for the wrestler. Additionally, a move on the ROPES card can be a Specialty move (denoted by a "(S)"), a move that leads to a pin attempt (denoted by a "P/A"), a submission move (denoted by a "*"), or a disqualification move (denoted by a "(DQ)").
